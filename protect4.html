Protect - P4: Prepare for, detect, and respond to data breaches.

The following questions are about data protection and privacy.

Is the organization aware of the potential impacts from breaches of personal data and does it have a response plan in place?

Does the organization:

1. Notify required parties of breaches of personal data, including data subjects and supervisory authorities (within 72 hours for supervisory authorities), when there is a high risk of impact to data subjects?

2. Provide required data breach notices using clear and plain language, giving the breach's nature and impact, the appropriate contact person, and the organization's remedy for the breach?

3. Have a process or technology to detect data breaches across all data stores in its control, including online, offline, and third party systems?

4. Maintain detailed records of data breaches, including their origin, impacts, and remedies?

5. Discuss, document, and apply lessons learned from data breaches?

6. Regularly update its data breach response procedures and technology?

7. Maintain metrics for how breaches of personal data are detected, remedied, and reported, such as operational impact and remediation efficiency?
