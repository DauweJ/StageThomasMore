Protect - P1: Data protection and privacy by design and default.

The following questions are about data protection and privacy.

Is the organization planning how to develop its technology, products, processes, and organizational structure with data protection and privacy as key components, and is it aware of the gaps for doing so?

Has the organization:

1. Established the ability to pseudonymize personal data?

2. Established a process to determine how much personal data is needed to perform the organization's operations?

3. Established process/personnel access controls (such as segregation of duties), where available technology would be insufficient to adequately protect privacy?

4. Established a policy/procedure to provide access to personal data using the principle of least privilege?

5. Established a policy/procedure to provide access to personal data using the principle of least privilege?

6. Integrated data protection and privacy as key components of relevant policies and processes?

7. Embedded data protection and privacy practices within the culture of the organization through ongoing training efforts and awareness programs?

Protect - P2: Secure personal data through encryption.

The following questions are about data protection and privacy.

Is the organization aware of technologies to encrypt personal data and has it encrypted some personal data, such as government identification numbers, birth dates, or banking numbers?

Has the organization:

1. Have a policy or procedure in place to define what personal data to encrypt, how to encrypt it, and the purpose of encryption?
